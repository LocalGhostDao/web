// 001 — Mission

A SEAMLESS
NATURAL
INTERFACE

Today's neural interfaces fail because the body rejects them. Rigid electrodes in soft tissue create scarring, inflammation, and signal decay within months. The fundamental problem has never been the electronics — it's the boundary.

We dissolve that boundary. Using living cells as the interface layer itself, Axo's biohybrid approach creates connections the nervous system recognises as self — stable, high-resolution, and lasting.

Near-term: restoring function for people with neurological disorders. Long-term: transforming how every human interacts with technology.

// 003 — The Science

BIOLOGY
AS THE
INTERFACE

The standard approach puts metal in tissue and hopes the body tolerates it — it doesn't, not indefinitely. Axo's biohybrid architecture places engineered living cells between the electrode and the nerve. The body doesn't reject them. It connects to them.

01 / 03

iPSC-Derived Cells

Induced pluripotent stem cells reprogrammed into myocytes — human muscle cells — that act as biological signal receptors. The body recognises them as self. No immune cascade. No foreign body response. Just integration.

02 / 03

Flexible Electrode Arrays

Custom multi-electrode arrays on substrates with elastic moduli matched to neural tissue. Mechanical mismatch is a primary driver of chronic inflammation — our devices bend, flex, and move with the body they're inside.

03 / 03

Biohybrid Integration

The cell layer forms synaptic connections with host nerve fibres on one side, couples electrically to the electrode array on the other. A living bridge between biology and machine — bidirectional, high-resolution, improving over time as tissue integrates.

// 004 — Technology

ENGINEERED
AT EVERY
LAYER

01

Materials Science

Novel substrates engineered for ultra-low mechanical stiffness — matching the elastic modulus of neural tissue to eliminate immune response and maximise implant longevity.

02

Genome Engineering

CRISPR-Cas9 editing of iPSC lines to optimise electrophysiological properties — tuning how cells receive and relay electrical signals with high fidelity.

03

ASIC Design

Custom silicon for high-density recording and stimulation in implantable form factors. Power-constrained, miniaturised, validated against IEC 60601 medical device standards.

04

In Vivo Validation

Long-duration animal studies demonstrating stable nerve integration and functional signal restoration. All biohybrid devices in our 28-day implant study showed nerve response. Controls showed none.

// 005 — Applications

RESTORING
FUNCTION.
EXPANDING
POSSIBILITY.

Every application starts from the same premise: give the nervous system a partner it can trust. Near-term that means medicine. Long-term that means reimagining what the human body can do.

Peripheral Nerve Restoration

Reconnecting severed peripheral nerves after amputation or injury — restoring sensory and motor function with a fidelity that rigid electrode arrays cannot sustain long-term.

Near-term Clinical

Neurological Disorders

Targeted neuromodulation for Parkinson's, epilepsy, and spinal cord injury — through interfaces stable enough for the chronic, long-duration implantation that treatment actually requires.

Clinical Development

Brain-Computer Interfaces

High-channel-count cortical recording with chronic stability. The bandwidth possible through biohybrid interfaces — with billions of synaptic connections — dwarfs what traditional electrode arrays can achieve.

Research Stage

Human Augmentation

The long horizon: seamless bidirectional communication between the nervous system and technology. The same biology that makes medicine possible makes this inevitable. We're building the foundation now.
